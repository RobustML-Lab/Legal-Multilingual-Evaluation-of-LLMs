The<br>regulation, effective January 1, 2021 (except<br>Article 87, effective upon entry into force),<br>updates the rules governing the Union Registry and<br>the European Union Transaction Log (EUTL).  **Key<br>Changes and Provisions:**  * **Harmonization with<br>Financial Market Legislation:** The regulation<br>aligns the Union Registry with  Directive<br>2014/65/EU (Markets in Financial Instruments<br>Directive) and Regulation (EU) No 596/2014 (Market<br>Abuse Regulation), classifying emission allowances<br>as financial instruments and requiring ISIN codes.<br>* **New Trading Period (2021 onwards):**  The<br>regulation addresses the new EU ETS period,<br>including:     * **Allowance Validity:**<br>Allowances issued from 2021 onwards will indicate<br>their creation period (ten-year intervals). This<br>information is only visible to account holders<br>where necessary for distinguishing allowances from<br>different phases.     * **Surrender<br>Restrictions:** Allowances can only be used for<br>emissions from the first year of their ten-year<br>issuance period.  Compliance calculation rules are<br>specified.     * **International Credits:** The<br>use of international credits is no longer<br>permitted from 2021, though provisions remain for<br>operations related to the 2013-2020 period.  *<br>**Registry System:**     * **Union Registry:**<br>Operated and maintained by the central<br>administrator, it ensures accurate accounting of<br>EU ETS transactions.  Technical requirements are<br>specified.     * **EUTL:** An independent<br>transaction log, also operated by the central<br>administrator, records and checks all<br>transactions.  Communication links between the<br>registry and EUTL are defined.     * **National<br>Administrators:** Each Member State designates a<br>national administrator responsible for account<br>holders within their jurisdiction.  Cooperation<br>between national and central administrators is<br>outlined.  * **Accounts:**     * **Account<br>Types:**  The regulation details account types<br>(e.g., operator holding accounts, aircraft<br>operator holding accounts, trading accounts) and<br>the unit types each can hold.     * **Account<br>Status:**  Account statuses (open, blocked,<br>closure pending, closed, excluded) are defined and<br>procedures for changing statuses are specified.<br>* **Account Opening and Updating:** Procedures for<br>opening and updating various account types are<br>detailed, including required information (Annex<br>III, IV, VI, VII, VIII) and verification<br>processes.  Refusal to open accounts or approve<br>representatives is addressed.     * **Authorised<br>Representatives:** Rules for authorized<br>representatives, including their access rights and<br>nomination procedures, are established.     *<br>**Account Closure:** Procedures for closing<br>accounts, including those with positive balances,<br>are outlined.     * **Suspension of Access:**<br>Grounds for suspending access to accounts are<br>defined, along with procedures and remedies.  *<br>**EU ETS Specifics:**     * **Verified<br>Emissions:** Procedures for submitting, verifying,<br>and correcting emissions data are detailed.<br>Blocking of accounts for failure to submit data is<br>addressed.     * **Compliance Status:**<br>Calculation of compliance status figures is<br>specified.     * **Transactions:** Rules for<br>allowance transfers, including execution timing<br>and reversal procedures, are established.  The<br>fungibility and finality of transactions are<br>emphasized.     * **Allowance Creation:**  The<br>process of creating allowances, including<br>assigning unique identification codes and ISIN<br>codes, is detailed.  Allowances from withdrawing<br>Member States are addressed.     * **Free<br>Allocation:** Procedures for free allocation of<br>allowances to stationary installations and<br>aircraft operators are defined, including the<br>handling of national allocation tables and<br>corrections for over-allocation.     *<br>**Auctioning:** Procedures for auctioning<br>allowances are detailed.     * **Surrender and<br>Deletion:** Procedures for surrendering and<br>deleting allowances are specified.   * **Linking<br>with Other Systems:** The regulation addresses the<br>implementation and suspension of linking<br>arrangements with other greenhouse gas emission<br>trading systems.  * **Technical Requirements:**<br>The regulation lays out technical requirements for<br>the Union Registry and EUTL regarding<br>availability, security, authentication, automated<br>checking, recording, and data reconciliation.<br>Data exchange and technical specifications are to<br>be provided by the Commission.  Record-keeping and<br>data protection are addressed, highlighting the<br>roles of the Commission and national<br>administrators as joint controllers of personal<br>data. Confidentiality rules are established,<br>specifying which entities can access data under<br>what circumstances.  Fee structures are outlined.<br>* **Transitional Provisions:**  The regulation<br>includes provisions for the continued use of<br>accounts and data from the previous system<br>(Regulation (EU) No 389/2013),  handling of Kyoto<br>units, and submission of new account information.<br>Amendments to Regulation (EU) No 389/2013 are<br>specified, and its repeal from 2021 (except for<br>operations related to the 2013-2020 period) is<br>stated.   In essence, this regulation<br>significantly overhauls the EU ETS registry<br>system, improving its security, efficiency, and<br>alignment with broader financial market<br>regulations while maintaining environmental<br>integrity.  Numerous annexes provide detailed<br>specifications and information requirements. |
